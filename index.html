How It Works

Import your season. Parents join in seconds. Alerts run themselves.

Three steps from spreadsheet chaos to a program your parents and volunteers can actually follow.

1

Import or build your schedule

Upload a spreadsheet or add events one at a time. Keep it private until you're ready, then publish a branded public page and ICS calendar feeds.

2

Parents join via QR code

Share a link at your parent meeting. Parents find their player, subscribe to updates, and view the schedule — no app download or account required.

3

Changes notify the right people

Edit a time, cancel a game, or open a volunteer slot — alerts go to parents, assigned volunteers, meal coordinators, and officials automatically.

Why SidelineOps HQ

When the schedule moves, communication breaks down.

Coaches already have calendars, group threads, and email lists — but a rain delay on Wednesday still means five separate messages to five different groups.

The problem
  • Schedule changes are easy to make, hard to communicate consistently.
  • Parents, players, volunteers, and meal coordinators get updated separately.
  • Volunteer slots go unfilled when games move or get added.
  • GroupMe is great for chatter — terrible as a schedule system of record.
  • Spreadsheets don't send alerts when cell B14 changes at 4 PM.
The SidelineOps HQ approach
  • One schedule update triggers targeted alerts to everyone affected.
  • Volunteer signup, assignments, and help-needed alerts in one place.
  • Public schedule page parents can bookmark — plus ICS for their calendar app.
  • GroupMe integration for teams that already live there.
  • Multi-team programs share events, call-ups, and team-specific times.
